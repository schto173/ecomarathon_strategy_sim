# Lessons ## 2026-06-20 — Model was too optimistic vs real-world (team feedback) **Correction:** The idealised physics underestimated three real losses. Calibrated to the team's Erfahrungswerte: - **Corners** slow the car far more than the grip-limited cap implied (the default μ=0.8 let it carry ~41 km/h through the tightest bend, which never bound). Reality: tight corners (R≈20 m) are taken at ~18–20 km/h (enter 26 → exit 18), while large / downhill corners are fine. → lowered effective corner grip (`mu_lat` 0.8→0.20, `corner_safety` 0.9→0.80) so the radius-dependent cap bites on tight corners and leaves large ones alone. The √R law already "adjusts to the corner radius." - **Engine restart** costs ~5× the idealised default → `restart_fuel_g` 0.02→0.10. - **Uphill** bleeds more speed than pure gravity+drag → added `grade_uphill_factor` (1.15) applied only to climbs (driveline/scrub losses under load). **Pattern / how to apply:** idealised first-principles physics is optimistic; real vehicles lose more in corners, transients (restarts), and under load. Expose these as tunable loss factors with conservative defaults, and *calibrate to measured/experience values* rather than trusting textbook coefficients. When a parameter (μ) stops binding, that's a red flag the model is too generous there. Keep effective parameters (effective grip ≠ tyre grip) and label them as such.
